Clark Street Play Community Garden

Get Involved

Welcome to a community maintained green space for neighbours to relax, gather, walk, picnic, play, pray, grow food, and support biodiversity.

Over the past five years, neighbours have planted native trees, berry bushes, vegetables, and pollinator-friendly flowers — creating a growing urban nature reserve.

Join us on the second Sunday of every month to do some communal planting and gardening from 12-3PM. Tea flasks and biscuits welcome!

Community Planting & Park in Bloom

Growing Biodiversity

  • Bird species include 30+ nesting sparrows (Red List species) blue tits, great tits, wrens, robins, jays and a sparrowhawk
  • Wildflower species including pink sorrel, pink ivy-leaved speedwell, birds eye speedwell, red deadnettle, grenn alkanet,
  • Insect species including elephant hawk moth caterpillars, ashy mining bees, spring epistrophe,

Learn more about how to support and safeguard urban green spaces for nature and people here: Avon Wildlife Trust .
