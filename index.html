This is not derelict land.

Clark Street Park is a community-maintained green space used daily by residents for walking, children’s play, prayer, picnics, and gardening.

Over the past five years, neighbours have planted native trees, berry bushes, vegetables, and pollinator-friendly flowers — creating a growing urban nature reserve.

Growing Biodiversity

  • 30+ nesting sparrows (Red List species)
  • Blue tits, great tits, wrens, robins, jays
  • Regular visits from a sparrowhawk
  • Rare grasshopper species
  • Elephant hawk moth caterpillars

The Park in Bloom

Why We’re Concerned

A proposal is being discussed to build HMOs extending the Clark Street terrace into the park. This would remove established trees, destroy nesting habitat, and permanently reduce one of the few accessible green spaces in this low-income neighbourhood.

Once lost, this space cannot be replaced.
